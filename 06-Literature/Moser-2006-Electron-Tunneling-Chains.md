# Electron Tunneling Through Protein Chains in Mitochondrial Respiratory Complexes

## Citation

**Authors**: Christopher C. Moser, P. Leslie Dutton
**Journal**: *Biochimica et Biophysica Acta — Bioenergetics* (various, 1992–2006)
**Key paper**: Moser CC, Keske JM, Warncke K, Farid RS, Dutton PL. *Nature* 355:796-802 (1992)

## Key Findings

- Established that electron transfer rates in proteins follow a simple exponential distance dependence — tunneling through protein medium with a decay constant of ~1.4 Å⁻¹
- Mitochondrial respiratory chain complexes (I–IV) are organized with redox cofactors spaced at tunneling-accessible distances (~14 Å edge-to-edge maximum)
- The protein matrix functions as a structured tunneling medium, not merely an inert scaffold — specific pathways through bonded and non-bonded contacts modulate transfer rates
- Free energy and reorganization energy, along with distance, determine electron tunneling rates — the Marcus-Dutton framework unifies quantum tunneling with thermodynamic driving forces
