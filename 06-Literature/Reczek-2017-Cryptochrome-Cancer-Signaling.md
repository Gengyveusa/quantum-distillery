# Reczek 2017 — Cryptochrome & Cancer Signaling

> Cryptochrome proteins and their role in cancer cell signaling pathways.

## Key Findings

- **CRY1/CRY2 dysregulation in cancer:** Cryptochrome proteins are aberrantly expressed in multiple cancer types including breast, colorectal, and hepatocellular carcinoma. Overexpression of CRY1 correlates with poor prognosis in several solid tumors.
- **ROS and redox interaction:** Cryptochrome interacts with ROS and redox signaling pathways relevant to quantum spin states. The FAD cofactor in cryptochrome generates radical pairs whose spin dynamics are sensitive to the cellular redox environment — cancer's elevated ROS alters these spin states.
- **Circadian disruption:** Cryptochrome mutations and dysregulation lead to circadian disruption, which is independently associated with increased cancer risk. Shift workers with chronic circadian disruption show elevated cancer incidence (WHO Group 2A carcinogen classification for night shift work).
- **Mechanistic link to spin coherence:** Provides the mechanistic bridge between Architecture 4 (cryptochrome spin coherence) and cancer biology — cancer amplifies spin-dependent signaling through elevated ROS and altered radical pair dynamics.

## Architecture Mapping

- **Architecture:** Cryptochrome Spin Coherence (Architecture 4)
- **Cancer signature:** Amplified — CRY overexpression + ROS-altered spin states
- **Aging signature:** Not primary focus of this paper
