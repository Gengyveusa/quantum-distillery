# Evidence for Wavelike Energy Transfer Through Quantum Coherence in Photosynthetic Systems

## Citation

**Authors**: Gregory S. Engel, Tessa R. Calhoun, Elizabeth L. Read, Tae-Kyu Ahn, Tomáš Mančal, Yuan-Chung Cheng, Robert E. Blankenship, Graham R. Fleming
**Journal**: *Nature* 446:782-786 (2007)
**DOI**: 10.1038/nature05678

## Key Findings

- Two-dimensional electronic spectroscopy revealed long-lived quantum coherence in the Fenna-Matthews-Olson (FMO) bacteriochlorophyll complex at physiological temperatures
- Coherence lifetimes exceeded 660 fs at 277 K — far longer than expected for a warm, wet biological environment
- Energy transfer between chromophores proceeds via wavelike quantum mechanical pathways rather than classical incoherent hopping
- The protein scaffold appears to actively protect quantum coherence, suggesting biological systems have evolved to exploit quantum effects
