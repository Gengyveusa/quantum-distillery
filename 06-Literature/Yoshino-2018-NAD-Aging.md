# 📄 Yoshino et al. 2018 — NAD+ Intermediates in Aging

**Citation:** Yoshino J et al. *Cell Metab* 27:513-528 (2018).

**Key Finding:** NAD+ declines ~50% between ages 40–60, directly impacting sirtuin activity, mitochondrial function, and DNA repair capacity. NMN supplementation partially restores NAD+ levels in aged mice.

**Relevance:** Direct evidence for aging-decoherence through the NADH/NAD+ metabolite axis — supports Probius QES NADH readout as an aging biomarker. The magnitude of decline (50%) provides quantitative grounding for the QPI aging trajectory.
