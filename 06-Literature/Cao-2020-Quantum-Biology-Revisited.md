# Quantum Biology Revisited

## Citation

**Authors**: Jianming Cao, Richard J. Cogdell, Dwayne F. Coker, Hong-Guang Duan, Jürgen Hauer, Ulrich Kleinekathöfer, Thomas L.C. Jansen, Tomáš Mančal, R.J. Dwayne Miller, Jennifer P. Ogilvie, Valentyn I. Prokhorenko, Thomas Renger, Howe-Siang Tan, R. Tempelaar, Michael Thorwart, Erling Thyrhaug, Sebastian Westenhoff, Donatas Zigmantas
**Journal**: *Science Advances* 6:eaaz4888 (2020)
**DOI**: 10.1126/sciadv.aaz4888

## Key Findings

- Comprehensive reassessment of quantum effects in biology across photosynthesis, enzyme catalysis, olfaction, and magnetoreception — covering all four architectures relevant to the Quantum Distillery framework
- Confirmed that quantum coherence in photosynthetic complexes is real but its functional significance for energy transfer efficiency remains debated — coherence may be a byproduct rather than a driver
- Strong evidence for quantum tunneling in enzyme catalysis (hydrogen and electron transfer) with clear functional consequences for reaction rates
- Radical pair magnetoreception remains the most compelling example of a macroscopic quantum biological phenomenon — spin coherence times sufficient for magnetic compass function
- Identified key challenges: distinguishing genuinely quantum effects from classical noise, developing better experimental probes, and establishing causal links between quantum phenomena and biological function
