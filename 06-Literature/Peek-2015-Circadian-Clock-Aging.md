# Peek 2015 — Circadian Clock & Aging

> Circadian clock deterioration in aging, with focus on cryptochrome and PER protein decline.

## Key Findings

- **Age-related CRY decline:** CRY1 and CRY2 expression decreases progressively with age in multiple tissues. This decline correlates with loss of circadian rhythm amplitude and phase stability.
- **Circadian decoherence and mitochondrial dysfunction:** Age-related circadian disruption is linked to mitochondrial dysfunction — declining CRY expression impairs the clock-mitochondria feedback loop, reducing NAD+ cycling efficiency and Complex I activity.
- **Spin coherence aging signature:** The decline in cryptochrome expression with age implies reduced radical pair formation and spin coherence capacity — consistent with Architecture 4's aging↓ decoherence prediction.
- **Cross-architecture implications:** Circadian clock deterioration affects not only spin coherence but also NADH/NAD+ cycling (Architecture 3, ETC) and redox homeostasis (Architecture 2, tunneling via GSH), suggesting cryptochrome decline has cascading effects across the quantum protection framework.
