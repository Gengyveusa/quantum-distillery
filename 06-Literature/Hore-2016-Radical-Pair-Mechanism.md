# The Radical Pair Mechanism of Magnetoreception

## Citation

**Authors**: Peter J. Hore, Henrik Mouritsen
**Journal**: *Annual Review of Biophysics* 45:299-344 (2016)
**DOI**: 10.1146/annurev-biophys-032116-094545

## Key Findings

- Cryptochrome proteins in avian retinas form radical pairs upon blue-light absorption, with spin states sensitive to Earth's magnetic field — enabling biological magnetoreception
- Spin coherence in FAD-tryptophan radical pairs persists for microseconds at physiological temperatures — orders of magnitude longer than expected for a biological system
- The protein environment shields radical pair spin states from decoherence, maintaining quantum superposition long enough for magnetic field sensitivity
- Behavioral and neurobiological evidence in migratory birds confirms functional dependence on the radical pair mechanism — quantum effects directly influence organism behavior
