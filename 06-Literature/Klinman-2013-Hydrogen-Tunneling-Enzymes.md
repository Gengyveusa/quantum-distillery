# Hydrogen Tunneling in Enzyme Catalysis

## Citation

**Authors**: Judith P. Klinman
**Journal**: *Journal of the American Chemical Society (JACS)* 135:2939-2942 (2013)
**DOI**: 10.1021/ja312191b

## Key Findings

- Demonstrated temperature-independent kinetic isotope effects (KIEs) in enzyme-catalyzed hydrogen transfer reactions, providing direct evidence for quantum mechanical tunneling
- Protein conformational sampling creates optimized donor-acceptor distances (DAD) that facilitate tunneling — the enzyme active site acts as a quantum-tuned environment
- Tunneling contributions are not incidental but essential for catalytic efficiency — enzymes have evolved to exploit quantum mechanical hydrogen transfer
- Mutations that disrupt DAD sampling impair tunneling and reduce catalytic rates, confirming the functional role of quantum effects

## Relevance to Quantum Distillery

- Provides mechanistic basis for how metabolic enzymes (including those processing NADH, FAD, GSH) depend on quantum tunneling for normal function
- Cancer cells with hyperactive metabolism may show accelerated tunneling rates (cancer↑) through upregulated enzyme activity and altered active-site dynamics
- Aging-related protein misfolding and oxidative damage disrupts DAD geometry, impairing tunneling efficiency (aging↓)
- QPI model captures tunneling status indirectly through metabolite flux ratios (NADH/NAD+, GSH/GSSG) that reflect enzyme tunneling throughput
